# Downloaded Papers

## Core Papers on Linear Representations + Compositionality

1. **The Linear Representation Hypothesis and the Geometry of Large Language Models** (`2311.03658_park2023_linear_rep.pdf`)
   - Authors: Park, Choe, Veitch
   - Year: 2023
   - arXiv: 2311.03658
   - Why relevant: Foundational formalization of linear representations, causal inner product

2. **The Geometry of Categorical and Hierarchical Concepts in LLMs** (`2406.01506_park2024_geometry_categorical.pdf`)
   - Authors: Park, Choe, Jiang, Veitch
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: 2406.01506
   - Why relevant: Extends to vectors, proves hierarchical orthogonality, polytope representations

3. **Function Vectors in Large Language Models** (`2310.15213_todd2023_function_vectors.pdf`)
   - Authors: Todd, Li, Sen Sharma, Mueller, Wallace, Bau
   - Year: 2023
   - arXiv: 2310.15213
   - Why relevant: Directly tests semantic vector composition of function vectors

4. **How Do Language Models Compose Functions?** (`2510.01685_khandelwal2025_compose_functions.pdf`)
   - Authors: Khandelwal, Pavlick
   - Year: 2025
   - arXiv: 2510.01685 (note: original download at 2502.07419 was incorrect)
   - Why relevant: Compositional vs. direct mechanisms; composition is fallback when no linear shortcut exists

5. **Linear Correlation in LM's Compositional Generalization and Hallucination** (`2502.04520_peng2025_linear_correlation.pdf`)
   - Authors: Peng, An, Hao, Dong, Shang
   - Year: 2025
   - arXiv: 2502.04520 (note: original download at 2501.01539 was incorrect)
   - Why relevant: Linear transformations between related knowledge; two-criteria test for compositionality (correlation + precision)
   - Code: github.com/KomeijiForce/LinCorr
